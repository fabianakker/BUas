Erasmus Bridge "The Swan" (1996)
  • 802m long, 139m asymmetrical pylon creates dramatic vertical in horizontal landscape
  • Iconic silhouette often referred to as "the Swan", looks white but blue
  • Connects north/south Rotterdam over the Nieuwe Maas
Kop van Zuid
  • Former docklands transformed to mixed-use district
  • Cruiseships would dock here Holland Amerika Lijn - Hotel New York
  • Multiple musea World Art Museum (100.000 objects) and Photo museum
üìç SKETCH LOCATION 2: Geldersekade Views
Cube Houses (1984, Piet Blom)
  • Houses tilted 45¬∞ to create "forest" Blaakse Forest
  • 38 cubes maximize density while maintaining individuality
  • Only 25% usable floor space - form creates constraints
Oude Haven & Witte Huis
  • Oldest harbor (1350s) - only pre-war remnant
  • Witte Huis (1898): Europe's first high-rise, 43m Art Nouveau survivor
  • Temporal layering: historic water meets ultramodern surroundings
üìç SKETCH LOCATION 3: Rotterdam Blaak
Markthal (2014, MVRDV)
  • Horseshoe solves weather protection market + 228 residential integration
  • 11,000m¬≤ ceiling artwork "Cornucopia" - 2 footbal fields - largest in Netherlands
  • Largest glass facade in Europe
Central Library (2007)
  • Yellow ventilation pipes celebrated, not hidden - "honest architecture"
  • Terraced interior creates natural gathering spaces and sight lines
Grote Kerk
  • Only medieval building in Rotterdam (rebuilt post-1940)
  • Gothic verticality vs horizontal modernism = visual rhythm
